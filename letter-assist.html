We are committed to protecting your privacy. LetterAssist is designed with privacy in mind — we collect the minimum data necessary to provide the service, and we never store the original content of your letters.

  • Document images: Photos or PDFs you scan are processed on your device and never uploaded
  • Personal identity: No login, registration, or personal details are required

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information solely to:

  • Provide the service: Process documents and generate AI interpretations
  • Manage credits: Track your credit balance and process purchases
  • Deliver notifications: Send local deadline reminders you set
  • Improve the App: Analyze anonymous usage patterns to improve quality and performance
  • Prevent abuse: Protect against unauthorized use of the service

We do NOT use your information for: advertising or marketing, selling to third parties, user profiling or behavioral targeting, or training AI models with your document content.

3. How Document Processing Works

Understanding exactly what happens to your data:

  1. On Your Device: You capture or import a document. Apple's Vision framework extracts text from the image entirely on your device. The original image is not uploaded anywhere.
  2. Transmitted to Our Server: The extracted text is sent via an encrypted connection to our Firebase Cloud Function server.
  3. Processed by AI: Our server forwards the text to OpenAI's API for interpretation. The text is processed and an interpretation is returned.
  4. Returned to You: The structured interpretation is sent back to your device. You may choose to save it locally.
  5. Not Retained: The original letter text is not stored on our servers or by the Cloud Function after processing is complete.

  • Privacy policy: apple.com/privacy

5. Data Storage and Security

5.1 On-Device Storage

  • Interpretation results: Stored locally using Apple's SwiftData framework in an encrypted SQLite database
  • Reminders: Stored locally on your device
  • Preferences: Stored in iOS UserDefaults on your device
  • No cloud sync: Local data is not synced to any cloud service

5.2 Server-Side Storage

  • Firestore: Stores only your anonymous user ID, credit balance, usage count, and timestamps
  • No letter content: Original document text is never stored server-side
  • Security rules: Firestore access is restricted — users can only read their own data; only server functions can write

5.3 Security Measures

  • All network communication uses TLS/HTTPS encryption
  • The OpenAI API key is stored server-side and never exposed to client devices
  • Firebase Security Rules prevent unauthorized data access
  • Anonymous authentication means no passwords or credentials are stored
  • iOS app sandboxing isolates app data from other applications
